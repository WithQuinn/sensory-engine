Your travel moments now come to life with more emotionally resonant, detailed narratives.

## What's New

### ✨ Emotion-Aware Storytelling

We've enhanced how Quinn captures the feeling of your travel moments. Narratives now automatically adapt to match your emotion:

- **Awe-inspiring moments** get reverent, expansive language that captures significance
- **Joyful experiences** come through bright and energetic, like you're reliving them
- **Peaceful times** flow gently with soft, calming details
- **Exciting adventures** pulse with quick energy and vivid specifics

**Example - Before:**
> "We visited Senso-ji Temple. It was amazing and unforgettable."

**Example - After (Awe):**
> "Morning at Senso-ji Temple. The weight of history—1,400 years of prayers and pilgrims. Moments like these remind you what matters."

---

### 🎯 More Specific, Less Generic

Travel narratives now avoid clichés and generic descriptions. Instead of "breathtaking" or "hidden gem," you get specific observations:

- ✅ "The monk's voice echoed across empty stone"
- ✅ "Incense smoke curling through morning light"
- ✅ "Max's wide-eyed wonder at the dragon fountain"

**What we removed:** All the overused travel phrases (hidden gem, bucket list, off the beaten path, etc.)

**What we added:** Specific sensory details that actually capture your unique moment

---

### 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Natural Companion Integration

Your travel companions are now woven naturally into your story:

**Before:**
> "Mom enjoyed the temple. Max enjoyed it too."

**After:**
> "Mom stood silent, taking it in. Max couldn't stop asking about the giant lantern—that kind of wonder you can't teach."

Each companion gets their own moment based on:
- Their age (kids vs adults get different details)
- The emotion you felt
- Their relationship to you

---

### 📱 Better Standalone Narratives

Your medium-length narratives (2-3 sentences) now work perfectly when shared, even without the photo:

**Structure:**
1. **Hook:** Sensory detail that draws you in
2. **Moment:** What actually happened
3. **Emotion:** How it felt

**Example:**
> "Golden light filtered through temple gates, casting long shadows across ancient stone. We walked slowly, breathing in incense and quiet—three strangers in a place that felt like home. That kind of peace you can't plan for."

This reads like a journal entry, not a caption.

---

### 📚 Story Arc for Full Narratives

Your longer narratives (journal-length) now follow a complete story structure:

1. **Setting:** Where, when, who was there
2. **Build:** The experience unfolding
3. **Peak:** The emotional high point
4. **Reflection:** What it meant

**Length:** 150-200 words (perfect for travel blogs, journals, or sharing with family)
